Tools and Stuff

these are the (very) basics we find super handy in the garden

Gardening gloves

An absolute essential to avoid being pricked, stung or goodness knows what else... Any colour or type will do, though heavier duty is handy for those pesky roses.

Bucket or Trug

Something to put your bits and pieces in so you can carry stuff around the garden and take waste to the bin. If it holds water you can use it to soak plant pots or to carry water around the garden instead of a watering can.

Watering can

If your garden hose isn't long enough or you need a more delicate touch. Ideally, one that you can just about carry when it's full, fitted with a rose (that's the bit at the end of the spout).

Trowel

Super useful for digging holes for planting, unless you like scrabbling with your hands in the earth. Any shape that fits comfortably in your hand will do.

Spade

Particularly good for digging larger holes and also for creating borders. Plus, once you have cut the grass, good for edging those borders and lopping off the longer bits of grass that have escaped the mower.

Fork

Great for digging over borders or beds to get rid of clods and mix in manure. Get big or small fork, or both.

Secateurs

For pruning, dead heading and even cutting string or wire. Go for ones that fit in your hand otherwise they will be painful to use for any length of time.

Bread knife

Honestly, kid you not. A cheap, serrated edge bread knife for cutting through tough roots, dividing root balls, etc. Keep it in the shed, though.

Scoop

Something to scoop compost, soil, sand, grit and chicken manure pellets. Kids sand toys work quite well, although plastic tubs and washing liquid bottles cut down will also do the trick.

Shears

You will only need these to start off with if you have a mature garden with lots of big shrubs.

Lawn Mower

Unless you like using scissors to cut your rapidly growing grass. Wee electric Flymo or heavier petrol - anything will do.
