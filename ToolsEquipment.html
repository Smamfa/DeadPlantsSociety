Tools and Stuff

The Absolute Basics to Keep Plants Alive

Gardening gloves

An absolute essential unless you have no cats around or you don't mind cat poo under your fingernails... Any colour or type will do, though heavier duty is handy for those pesky roses.

Bucket

TSomething to put your bits and pieces in so you can carry stuff around the garden and take waste to the bin. If it holds water you can use it to soak plant pots or to carry water around the garden instead of a watering can.

Watering can

One that you can just about carry when it's full, fitted with a rose.

Trowel

Useful for digging holes for plants. Any shape that fits comfortably in your hand will do.

Spade

Particularly good for digging larger holes and also for creating borders. Plus, once you have cut the grass, good for edging those borders and lopping off the longer bits of grass that have escaped the mower.

Fork

Great for digging over borders or beds to get rid of clods and mix in manure. Get big or small fork, or both.

Secateurs

For pruning, dead heading and even cutting string or wire. Go for ones that fit in your hand otherwise they will be painful to use for any length of time.

Bread knife

Honestly, kid you not. A cheap, serated edge bread knife for cutting through tough roots, dividing root balls, etc. Keep it in the shed, though...

Scoop

Something to scoop compost, soil, sand, grit and chicken manure pellets. Kids sand toys work quite well as do plastic tubs and cut down washing liquid bottles.

Shears

You will only need these to start off with if you have a mature garden with lots of big shrubs.

Lawn Mower

GUnless you like using scissors to cut your rapidly growing grass. Wee electric Flymo or heavier petrol - anything will do.
